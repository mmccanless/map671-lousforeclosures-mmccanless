Foreclosures per 1000 people in Louisville, KY

Authored by: mmccanless

Foreclosures are an important indicator of housing insecurity. Across the city of Louisville, majority non-white neighborhoods-- with Black or African-American neighborhoods being the primary non-white demographic-- have higher rates of foreclosure. In instances where majority white neighborhoods experience high rates of foreclosure such as the two tracts in Louisville's West End, these sites should be monitored for intensifiying processes of gentrification. See attached for a geoJSON of foreclosures in Louisville.

Table of census tracts with highest rates of foreclosure

Map details

This map was made using data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Jefferson County circuit court. It shows rates of foreclosure per 1000 people between the years of 2016-2020. The inset maps show rates of foreclosure in majority white and majority non-white neighborhoods in Louisville, KY.

University of Kentucky New Maps Plus, Fall 2021.
